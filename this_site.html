Sketching

Every project starts with an idea and this portfolio is no different. Even if it was created for the purpose of showcasing my skill for future employers, I knew from the very beginning that I wanted to make this my own. More than just literally being my portfolio.

After putting a few thoughts on the table, I came to the idea of making my portfolio resemble a social media platform. More specifically, I wanted it to resemble an artistic platform like Deviantart or Instagram - with a profile picture, bio and everything.
And so, I started sketching out my idea, working to make the inspiration clear, but the look unique. Once I was done and happy I had to bring in some colors.
I went through a few palettes, making moodboards for each of them. Then I had to decide which one to continue with. It took a bit of asking around, but somewhere I had a feeling which one I'd be using. The bluish and purple colors of this palette was much more relaxing and easy on the eyes than the others I had thrown around. But my gut feeling had definitely came from the image that I got the colors from. To this day it's been one of my favorite photographs that I've taken!
And like that, I was ready to take the project to the next step.

Prototype

With my idea and colors ready, it was time to take the project to the digital world.

I did this with help from Adobe XD, going through three stages of the design. These were of course a wireframe in grayscale to get a feel for the layout of the portfolio and where the text and images would be. A mockup where I added colors and the most important images to the wireframe until I was happy with it. And finally... the big step of turning it into a functional prototype.
Here, XD becomes very useful with its prototype tool. Not only could I make the different pages look exactly how I wanted them for the actual website, but I could also link them together to look work like an actual website!
It really looks like a mess of tentacles, but it works.
But after hours of connecting everything together, I had a fully working prototype of what my website would look like and how it would work (in a mobile size, but good enough).
With that, I was ready to start coding the actual website. Try out the prototype

Coding

Now was the time to actually create the website that you're looking through right now!

Going in, I already knew which new challenges I was going to be facing. But I also knew how I wanted to approach them to get through them the best way possible.
Everything from a root with all my colors and fonts, to creating a blank page to work off of with every new page - I felt prepared. And of course I had to change the look of the website to fit every different screen size. Mobile all the way to desktop.
However, all thes things were tools and tricks that I had worked with before during my time at Zealand...
The real test of my skill came into play when I had to learn the javascript used for, well, the pages you're looking through right now!
I knew from the start that I wanted a read more option on these pages, before I had to make 10 more pages for each project (whew). But it turned out that the one small button that you see below, is a bit more complicated than it looks. Well, it uses javascript... which I had never dared to touch before now. But now I had to push myself to try. I wasn't about to step back already.
And with help from W3schools I was quickly able to find a simple solution to make this button. But then came the next problem. What if I have multiple texts that need to be read more one at a time? All the new buttons kept opening the same first text. So, I had to experiment.
Going back and forth with W3schools tryit tool, I slowly learnt what each part did. And then it suddenly clicked - and worked! Now, each button and function has its own number to distinguish them.
It's far from clean, but for a javascript newbie, I'd like to say that I'm proud. And I'm ready to learn more of it in the future. Hopefully with a step ahead now that I've tried it before.
